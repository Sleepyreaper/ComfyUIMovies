An epic, driving cinematic action chase score, fully instrumental, about 60
seconds, tense and propulsive with a heroic heart. A hybrid of pounding
orchestra and modern electronic textures, colored with East-Asian instruments
(deep taiko drums, plucked koto, a breathy bamboo flute) over a moody neon-city
atmosphere. Build it as a story of a hunted animal outrunning a machine through a
rain-soaked night:

(0–8s) a low, ominous pulse and a lone breathy flute, tense and watchful, the
calm before the chase.

(8–20s) sudden explosive percussion — taiko and driving electronic drums kick in
with a relentless galloping rhythm, urgent staccato strings and a pulsing synth
bassline, adrenaline surging.

(20–36s) the chase intensifies, brass stabs and a rising string ostinato trading
with sharp percussion hits, danger and momentum climbing, a few breathless
half-second drops for near-misses.

(36–48s) the music launches into a soaring, triumphant crescendo — big heroic
brass and full strings over the taiko groove, the leap-and-escape high point,
goosebump energy.

(48–60s) it releases and resolves — the percussion falls away to a warm, proud
sustained string chord and the lone flute returning, breathless and free, fading
softly on a hopeful, unresolved shimmer.

Rich, cinematic, high-energy, emotional, modern hybrid film-score. Strong rhythmic
drive. No vocals, no lyrics.
